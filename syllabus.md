# Introduction to Democratic Principles - Syllabus

## Course Description
This course provides an introduction to the foundational principles of democratic governance, including liberty, equality, justice, representation, and civic participation. Students will explore historical and contemporary contexts, analyze case studies, and develop critical thinking about democratic systems.

## Learning Objectives
1. Understand core democratic values and their philosophical origins.
2. Analyze the structures and functions of democratic institutions.
3. Evaluate challenges to democracy in the modern world.
4. Develop skills in civil discourse and evidence-based argumentation.

## Course Structure
- Module 1: Origins of Democratic Thought
- Module 2: Institutions and Processes
- Module 3: Contemporary Challenges
- Module 4: Civic Engagement and Future Directions

## Assessment
- Participation: 20%
- Reading Responses: 30%
- Midterm Project: 20%
- Final Essay: 30%

## Required Texts
- Dahl, R. A. (1998). *On Democracy*. Yale University Press.
- Held, D. (2006). *Models of Democracy*. Stanford University Press.
- Additional readings provided in each module.
